Raw notes, posting because the alternative is never posting.

The phrase *agents across departments* sounds like a marketing line and it kind of is, but
under it there's a shape I keep noticing:

- The interesting agent in a company isn't the one that talks to *customers*. It's the
  one that talks to *another team's tooling*.
- 80% of "I want an agent for X" is "I want a coworker who reads docs and clicks
  buttons". The hard part is the docs and the buttons existing, not the agent.
- Most "deterministic AI" work I'm doing is really *evaluation infrastructure* — scenarios,
  persona replay, judge tuning. The agent is the easy part. Knowing whether the agent is
  better than last week is the whole job.
