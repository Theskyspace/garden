My first founding-engineer chapter, and the one that taught me what scales cleanly and
what doesn't. Three years, 120K users, INR 1B+ in transaction value, 200+ brand
partners.

I built the backend from zero — Django + NestJS — covering both the B2C consumer app
and the B2B brand-partner side. On AWS: ECS, S3, RDS, PostgreSQL, the boring stack done
properly. Set up CI/CD on Jenkins + Docker + Makefiles back when "AI-generated yaml"
wasn't a thing.

Some of what I learned here became the post on [0 → 1M
transactions](/writing/zero-to-one-million-transactions) — what scaled, what we deferred
too long, what I'd do differently next time.

The unsung win: programmatic automation for design & marketing that cut 90% of manual
intervention. Wasn't engineering for engineering's sake — it bought the team months of
focus we wouldn't have otherwise had.
